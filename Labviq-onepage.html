What we do

The lab works. The record keeps up.

Every lab has the same gap. Materials arrive as physical objects, but their digital records depend on someone stopping to log them by hand. More often than not, that doesn't happen — and the record drifts out of step with what's actually on the shelf.

Labviq closes that gap. It captures materials at the point of intake and turns each one into a complete, verified record — automatically, as part of the normal workflow rather than a separate task.

The result is a living record of everything in the lab — searchable, connected to the work it supports, and kept current as materials are used and replaced.

Company

Labviq is a system of record for research materials in life-science labs. We sit at the seam between the physical and digital lab — the moment a material arrives, is stored, used or moved — and make that moment automatic.

Based in London and founded in 2026, Labviq builds granular, verified records of every reagent and sample a lab handles, captured at the point of intake and kept current across the material's lifecycle. The aim is end-to-end traceability — from supplier to result — that makes reproducible, auditable science the default rather than an administrative burden.
